Answer as much or as little as you like — even one word gets you three concepts. Each step below is optional and just sharpens the result. Tap the ? on any step if you're unsure, type your own words anywhere, or use the suggestion chips.

Step 1 of 4 · Your idea

What do you want tattooed?

The main subject of your tattoo — an animal, object, symbol, or scene. Just name it in a few words; you'll add detail on the next steps. This is the only step you need — even one word works.

Step 2 of 4 · Details

Add any details

Extra elements and composition: what surrounds the subject, its pose, framing, or props. Optional — but the more you add, the sharper the concept. Tap chips to stack ideas, or type your own; separate them with commas.

Step 3 of 4 · Style

Pick a style

The art style or technique your tattoo is drawn in — it changes the whole look. Pick one below, or type your own in the box (e.g. biomechanical, stick-and-poke, dark surrealism, sketchy grunge). Leave it on “Artist's choice” to let the AI decide.

Step 4 of 4 · Placement & finish

Where, and any finish?

Where the tattoo sits on the body and its overall finish. Placement shapes the proportions (a sleeve is tall, a sternum is wide); the finish sets the mood. Both optional — type your own placement or tap a chip, and pick any finishes that fit.
